D3 State Obesity

Inisghts of Correlations Between Health Risks, Age, and Income

Some interesting patterns and observations:

  • Here we see a positive correlation between the "Lacks Healthcare" and "In Poverty." Southeastern states tend to have higher rates of poverty and lack healthcare more than other states.
  • The age demographic falls mostly in between ages 35 and 40, with approximately 25% falling outside of this range.
  • We see a moderate, negative correlation between household income and lacking healthcare. So on average, households with lower income tend to have less healthcare.
  • Obesity has a moderate, positive correlation with poverty, none at all with age, and a negative correlation with income.
